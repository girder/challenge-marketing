What's a Challenge?

A Data-Driven Challenge is a problem with well-defined inputs and outputs to which competitors can submit results and/or the code to produce results to be automatically scored against a set of known "true" results. The submission closest to the true results wins!

Software is not Enough

The development of most novel software consists of four steps:

  1. Search literature in the problem domain to find the latest methods.
  2. Search the open source ecosystem for an implementation of a suitable algorithm.
  3. Customize the open source algorithm to meet your needs or build an implementation from scratch.
  4. Cross your fingers and hope the method is general enough for your use cases.

With data-driven challenges we can do better.

Instead of relying only on the published results, we can setup a streamlined, automated system to verify that any potential solution actually solves your problem on your data. Contact us to get started on creating your challenge today.

What's a Challenge?

A data-driven challenge (often called a Grand Challenge) is a structured "contest" that allows contestants to submit results or even executables, scripts, and/or code to be scored against the results or methods of other contestants. When challenge organizers chose representative datasets and define the problem well, data-driven challenges have the potential to bring the best method available to solve real-world probelms.

The result of good challenges is a win for both parties. Algorithm developers discover how methods work on real data, and challenge organizers find out what the field has to offer. Challenges provide an excellent start to many productive working relationships.

Why Challenges?

Modern scientific research software is often published with detailed analyses over the datasets to which the investigators had access. This often makes it difficult assess the utility of a given method for on your data. Businesses spend millions in research and development budgets in attempts to rework published methods on their specific data. We think there's a better way.

Data-driven challenges connect stakeholders with immediate, real problems with communities of algorithm developers eager to prove their methods' generality and applicability to new domains. Bringing these two complimentary groups in an automated web-based system enhances reproducibility and will save time and money.

How Does this Work?

The COVALIC system is built on the TangeloHub platform--an open source data and analytics platform made up of three major components: Tangelo, Girder, and Romanesco. To oversimplify things, data is submitted to Girder, processed with Romanesco, and visualized using Tangelo.
